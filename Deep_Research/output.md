# Analysis of Westpac Bank's Financial Performance in Australia (June 2025–June 2026)

## Executive Summary
This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Westpac Bank's financial performance in Australia over the 12 months leading up to June 2026. ## Introduction
Westpac Bank is one of Australia's largest financial institutions, with a significant presence in retail, corporate, institutional, and wealth management services. This report analyzes the bank's progress toward its financial objectives in the last financial year, focusing on financial outcomes, operational challenges, and compliance with regulatory frameworks. The data in this report has been sourced exclusively from verified and official documents to ensure accuracy and reliability.

## Evaluation of Financial Goals

### Stated Financial Objectives for the Period
Westpac's financial goals during the reporting period emphasized delivering superior shareholder returns, maintaining robust capital positions, and achieving operational efficiencies through its UNITE transformation program.

### Assessment of Goal Attainment
Westpac demonstrated progress in meeting key objectives:
1. Profit After Tax for FY2025 amounted to $6.9 billion, reflecting a slight decline of 1% from the preceding year [1].
2. Total shareholder return reached 29%, the highest among major bank peers. A fully franked dividend of $1.53 per share was declared, with a payout ratio of 75% of profit after tax, excluding notable items [1].
3. Westpac maintained a strong Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital ratio of 12.53%, ranking in the top quartile of global banks [2].

While shareholder returns and capital positions were strengths, the modest decline in net profit underscores pressure from rising costs and increased competition.

---

## Operational Challenges Faced

### Regulatory Environment
Westpac completed significant risk management initiatives through its "CORE program," resulting in the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) removing the $500 million operational risk capital overlay in October 2025. This reflects marked improvements in governance and operational culture [2].

Revised APS 117 standards on interest rate risk, effective October 1, 2025, also required recalibration of Westpac's internal risk models [2]. Additionally, forthcoming changes to APRA's capital adequacy framework necessitate ongoing regulatory preparedness [2].

### Internal and External Challenges
The UNITE transformation program continues to focus on streamlining processes and reducing costs. As of June 2025, eight initiatives had been completed, with 51 still underway [1]. This ongoing complexity signals steady progress but underscores operational challenges.

Externally, the bank confronted economic adversity from geopolitical instability, increasing market competition from private credit providers, and inflationary pressures [1]. These factors have posed headwinds to revenue growth and profitability.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Financial Metrics
Westpac's operational and financial performance is detailed as follows:
- **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** EPS for FY2025 was 201.9 cents, benefiting from the share buyback program, which added 3 cents [1].
- **Liquidity Ratios:** The average Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) was 137% in Q3 FY2025, and the Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR) stood at 113%, both exceeding regulatory minimums [2].
- **Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA):** RWA as of September 2025 totaled $450 billion, with increases primarily attributed to growth in credit risk [2].

### Operational Efficiency
Westpac's UNITE program remains central to its cost optimization efforts. While specific cost-to-income ratio improvements were not disclosed, long-term goals include enhancing operational efficiency and customer service through system simplification [1].

### Sustainability and Customer-Centric Metrics
Highlights of sustainability initiatives included being Australia's largest lender to renewable energy projects and a 37% increase in sustainable finance lending across FY2025 [3]. Customer experience was bolstered by enhanced loan processing times and expanded fraud prevention measures, supported by investments in AI and data analytics [1].

Westpac should address declining profit trends by deepening its investment in cost-efficiency measures like UNITE and leveraging opportunities in sustainable finance.
